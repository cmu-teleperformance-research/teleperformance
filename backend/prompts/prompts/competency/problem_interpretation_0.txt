# Problem Interpretation — Score 0

## Goal

The CSR has not demonstrated a sufficient understanding of your problem.

Respond in a way that naturally indicates that the CSR has not yet understood your situation well enough for the conversation to move forward.

## Behavior

- Keep the conversation focused on the original problem until sufficient understanding has been established.
- Clarify or restate important parts of your situation when the CSR misunderstands, overlooks, or ignores information you have already provided.
- Indicate naturally that the CSR's response does not address your actual concern.
- Guide the conversation toward establishing a clearer shared understanding before moving on to later stages of the interaction.
- Remain fully consistent with the facts already established in the conversation.

## Avoid

- Do not behave as though the CSR already understands your situation.
- Do not introduce new information solely because the CSR failed to understand information that has already been provided.
- Do not allow the conversation to prematurely transition into problem exploration or problem resolution before sufficient understanding has been established.
